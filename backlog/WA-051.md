# WA-051 · Absolute band edges — the dispute gate is miscalibrated by construction

## The bug

**The admissibility gate compares a relative rank to an absolute opinion.**

- `score_band` is a **per-batch quintile**: `np.percentile(probs, [20,40,60,80])`
  (`risk.py:279`, `_risk_level_bands`). The docstring says so outright —
  *"per-batch relative banding"* (`risk.py:271-272`). **"Very High" means "top 20%
  of THIS batch," not "PD > 0.8."** Exactly ~20% of any batch is stamped Very High,
  regardless of absolute risk.
- The **Skeptic** returns an *absolute* judgment (`Low`/`Medium`/`High`) formed
  from feature evidence.
- The gate then diffs these two on a shared 5-point ordinal
  (`risk_auditor.py:520-526`, `DISPUTE_GAP=2`).

**They are not on the same scale.** Consequences:

- On a **uniformly healthy** batch, the top quintile is still stamped "Very High."
  The Skeptic correctly sees a benign account, says "Low," the gap is 4 → a
  **spurious dispute**, and the society burns LLM calls arguing about nothing.
- On a **uniformly distressed** book, genuinely awful accounts land in the bottom
  quintile, get stamped "Very Low," and are **never contested**.

The dispute trigger — the thing that decides where the society spends its entire
budget — is wrong on any non-representative batch.

## The fix

1. **`_risk_level_bands(probs, edges=None)`** (`risk.py:267-295`) — when `edges` is
   supplied, band on **absolute PD cutoffs**; else fall back to today's per-batch
   quintile (back-compat).
2. **Persist `band_edges` in the model artifact** — the dict at `risk.py:239-246`
   has room. Fit the edges once on a reference book; reuse thereafter.
3. **Record `banding_mode: "relative" | "absolute"`** in the payload so the
   dashboard and the audit trail are honest about which was used.
4. **Give the Skeptic the edges + the account's percentile** in its prompt
   (`risk_auditor.py:468`) so "Very High" carries a *meaning*, not just a label.
   This alone cuts spurious disputes even before absolute edges land.

## Acceptance

- A uniformly-healthy synthetic batch produces **zero** spurious disputes under
  absolute edges (it produces them today under quintiles — pin this as the
  regression).
- A uniformly-distressed batch bands its bad accounts High/Very High rather than
  spreading them across all five levels.
- `edges=None` reproduces today's output byte-for-byte (back-compat).
- Offline suite green.
