# WASPADA · Brand research

## 1. What we're branding (product truths to build from)
- **A vigilance product.** An early-warning system (EWS) for loan collections — it watches a
  portfolio and tells a human where to look first.
- **A society, not a model.** The differentiator is six agents *arguing* — challenge, rebuttal,
  ruling — with a human holding the final gate. Debate/graph structure is brand-worthy material.
- **Audited trust.** Every claim cites evidence; every run leaves an audit trail. The brand should
  feel *accountable*, not magical.
- **Context:** Qwen Cloud hackathon judging (first impression = dashboard + demo video), Indonesian
  team, Alibaba/TDesign-blue UI already shipped.

## 2. The name — WASPADA is a genuine asset, use it harder
- **Meaning:** Indonesian/Malay for *vigilant, watchful, on guard* — awareness, protection,
  prudence. Exactly the product's job; no rename needed.
- **The killer connotation:** in Indonesia's official disaster-alert vocabulary (BMKG/PVMBG volcano
  & flood levels), **"Waspada" IS an alert level** — Level II on the national ladder
  *Normal → **Waspada** → Siaga → Awas*. The name literally means "the early-warning stage where
  you start paying attention." For an EWS product this is a story judges/users get in one sentence,
  and it's culturally distinctive (no Western risk brand can claim it).
- **Watch-outs:** *Waspada* is also a long-running Medan daily newspaper (waspada.co.id) — no
  product conflict, but don't imitate its red masthead; SEO for the bare word will be contested in
  Indonesian. "WASPADA" as an all-caps wordmark works well (3 syllables, symmetric, strong A's).
- **Tagline directions:** "Vigilance, argued." · "Early warning, audited." · "Waspada sebelum
  terlambat" (ID) · "The alert level for your portfolio."

## 3. Competitive / visual landscape (what to avoid & exploit)
- **Fintech-risk incumbents** (Feedzai, Sardine, Unit21, SEON): converge on **dark UIs + a single
  neon accent**, abstract "detection" motifs (radar, sonar, fish), and "trust blue" corporate sites.
  Sardine now literally brands as *"the agentic risk platform"* — the agentic framing is becoming
  table stakes; a node-graph motif alone won't differentiate for long.
- **Agentic-AI design trend 2026:** node-based/graph visuals are the emerging visual shorthand for
  agent systems — our WA-091 flow view is on-trend; the brand can own it by making the graph *a
  debate* (opposing nodes, a verdict) rather than a generic DAG.
- **Indonesian identity in tech:** batik/garuda motifs read as power + safeguarding, but crowded
  patterns fight minimal fintech UI; the research consensus is *minimalist reinterpretation* (a
  single geometric batik-derived line, not a pattern fill).
- **Our current state:** TDesign cloud-blue theme (#0052D9), a generic shield-with-dot favicon,
  no wordmark. The blue is safe and appropriate for the Alibaba context; the shield is the weakest
  part (reads as antivirus).

## 4. Three brand directions

### A. "Alert Level" — own the Indonesian EWS ladder  ⭐ recommended
- **Idea:** WASPADA = Level II. Brand the product as the national-alert metaphor: a **tiered
  signal** identity. The mark is an abstract *beacon/level indicator* (three stacked marks with the
  second lit — "you are at Waspada").
- **Palette:** keep cloud blue as ground; the alert ramp (green→amber→orange→red) already in the
  risk tokens becomes *brand-semantic* — the ladder is the identity.
- **Why it wins:** one-sentence story, culturally ownable, maps 1:1 to what the dashboard already
  shows (bands, alerts), zero conflict with the blue UI.

### B. "The Society" — the debate graph as the mark
- **Idea:** a minimal 3-node mark (challenger–defender–arbiter) with one edge bolded — the moment
  of ruling. Extends naturally into the WA-091 flow view, loading states, the demo video.
- **Risk:** generic-AI-network look if drawn lazily; "agentic" is getting crowded (Sardine).

### C. "Sentinel" — evolve the shield with an Indonesian eye
- **Idea:** keep protection symbolism but replace the antivirus-shield with a **stylized eye /
  garuda-wing single-line** (vigilance = watching). Minimal batik-derived geometry, one line weight.
- **Risk:** eyes are common in security branding (surveillance connotation to manage).

## 5. Recommendation
**Direction A (Alert Level), with B's graph as the *product* motif (WA-091), not the logo.**
- Keep: cloud-blue token system, sans type, semantic risk ramp (they already fit A).
- Replace: the shield favicon → a three-tier beacon mark (second tier lit, in brand blue).
- Wordmark: WASPADA all-caps, current sans at 750 weight is fine for the hackathon; a custom cut is
  post-submission polish.
- Copy: adopt the ladder language in UI/docs where honest — e.g. alert strip levels, the demo
  video's opening line: *"In Indonesia, 'Waspada' is the moment you start watching. So is this."*

## 6. Next steps (cheap → deep)
1. Owner picks a direction (A/B/C or a mix) — nothing below starts until then.
2. Mark sketches (SVG, 3 variants) + favicon swap — ~1 file each, token-colored.
3. tokens.css gets `--brand-mark` treatment + login/banner mark swap (WA-092 already made these

4. Demo video opening beat updated to the name story.
5. Post-hackathon: wordmark refinement, brand page in docs.
